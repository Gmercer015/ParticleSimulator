The Syntaf Particle Simulator

This Simulator is currently an ongoing project, originally used to test my knowledge and understanding of OpenGL and SFML, the project grew on me and I continued development. I plan to eventually include a build system or maybe even an executable under release, but for now it will remain a VS2012 project. To understand what the simulator currently does, here is a recent build, password: particles

Cool right? Well there's plenty more features to come!

What Exactly Is This Doing?

The simulator currently combines mouse input with newtons famous law (1/2)at^2 + v(i)t. Particles continually accelerate towards the mouse point while the user holds down LMB. Once released, a constant drag force is applied until the particles eventually grind to a halt, making for some pretty neat formations in the simulations!

Real World Applications

In it's current state, the simulator has very few, if any, real world applications. The project was originally a test of my openGL skills in the programmable pipeline, and grew into a hobby . Features are always being added and who knows, maybe in the short future the Particle simulator will have some real uses! Till then make sure to drop by and contribute, or at least say wave as you pass by.
